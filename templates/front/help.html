What is healthchecks?

Healthchecks is a cron monitoring web app that alerts you every time a cron job fails to execute.
To monitor this, you’re provided with an address that your cron job is supposed to ping each time it executes.
If a cron job fails to ping that address within the timeframe you have specified, healthchecks sends you an alert via email, slack or a number of other notification channels we’ve integrated.

FAQs

Do I need this?

Healthchecks will alert you when a cron job fails, so that you can fix it before it becomes a problem.
Like that important backup you've set to execute every Sunday afternoon, or that series of automatic downloads you have happening every night.

I could just check my email to see whether a email cron job executed or not, right?

Wrong. Checking your email and finding one from your cron job when it is successful is easy, but it can be difficult to tell when your jobs fail.
How do you confirm that an email was not sent when your inbox has thousands?
Healthchecks solves this problem by sending you a notification if your job hasn't executed when it should.
A second notification is also sent once your cron job begins executing again.

How do I get started?

On the health checks homepage, click on Log In.





You’ll be asked to provide an email address via which we can notify you once a cron job is “late”.







A log in link will be sent to your inbox. Click on the green “Log In” button, and you’ll be redirected to your User Dashboard.



To add a check, simply click on “Add Check”. This will create a default check, which you can proceed to edit. Click on “unnamed” to edit the name.









Hover over the check to reveal a grey settings button, and click on it to view a log of pings (which should currently be empty), to remove a check, but more importantly, to view “Usage Examples”.









The easiest usage example would be the one under “crontab”, but feel free to copy any of the others depending on which tool you are using to set up your cron jobs.

See here for how to set up cron jobs.

Back to our check, click under “Period” to configure how often you want Healthchecks to monitor the “health” of your cron jobs.





E.g. for a period of one hour and a grace time of 10 minutes, health checks will expect your cron job to ping the url you copied into cron tab every hour. The job will be late if it pings the address after an hour, but before the 10 minute grace time elapses. If both the period plus grace time elapse before a ping has been received, then the job is late, and healthchecks will send you an alert.

How do I set up Notification channels?

Notification of a failed check will primarily be via the email used to log in until other Integrations have been added. Under the Integrations Tab, click on “Add Integration” next to the medium you wish to add. This will redirect you to a screen with step by step instructions after which you should be able to receive notifications via this medium.

Healthchecks sent me an alert when I didn't expect it

If you receive an alert you didn't expect — if you know your job did not run too long for example — the first thing to do is view your recent ping history in your dashboard. Usually the explanation can be found in the history, but please hit us up on the chat if you have any questions.

How do I find out why my check failed?

Presently, you cannot. You have to manually investigate the causes from the machine you set up your cron jobs on. Stay tuned for this feature.

How can I check the status of my checks?

The badge next to each check on your dashboard indicates its current status. If the check's URL hasn't been hit yet, the badge will be gray. The badge will turn green after your job ping's its URL. If the check is late, the badge will turn orange. A check that is down will be red.

Can I re-organise checks in my dashboard?

By default, the dashboard displays checks alphabetically. However, we recommend that you tag each check. This makes it easier to search through them and/or categorize them.

Can I change a check's interval?

Easy. Head to your checks page, and choose a new interval.

How do I delete a snitch?

Click the settings icon next to your check, then "Remove".

Can't find the answer to your question?

Chat with us by clicking on the chat icon in the bottom right-hand corner of any healthchecks page. We're always ready to help!
